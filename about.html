About Rekha Singh

Two decades of quiet practice in Lucknow — listening before speaking, and offering guidance that respects your faith and your time.

Rekha Singh did not come to astrology as a profession; she came to it as a household tradition. Growing up in a family where the panchang sat on the puja shelf alongside the Ramayana, she learned early that astrology — done seriously — is a discipline of careful attention, not a parlour trick.

Her formal study began in her early twenties, under the guidance of teachers in Lucknow and Varanasi who insisted on first principles: a thorough grounding in Parashari classics, the patience to read a chart without rushing to predict, and the humility to admit when a reading needed more time. She later added training in Lal Kitab and the KP system, drawing on each tradition where it offered genuine insight.

For the past twenty years, Rekha ji has practised from her home in Indira Nagar, Lucknow. Her seekers come from across Uttar Pradesh — Kanpur, Varanasi, Gorakhpur, Prayagraj — and increasingly from Delhi, Mumbai, and the Indian diaspora abroad. They return, often year after year, for the same reason: she takes the time, and she does not sell fear.

Her philosophy is straightforward. Astrology is a guide, not a verdict. A chart describes patterns and tendencies; it does not erase free will. Where a remedy is needed, it should be dignified and proportionate — a mantra, a daan, a small puja — never an expensive yantra pushed on a worried family. Seekers leave with clarity, not dread.

She works in Hindi and English, in person at Indira Nagar or over WhatsApp video for those farther away. Consultations are unhurried by design. If a chart needs more study, she will say so and ask you to return — there is no theatre of instant pronouncement.

“A kundali is not a sentence. It is a letter — and our work, together, is to read it well.”

A Note from Rekha ji

“When you come to me with a question, you are also coming with a fear. My work is to read your chart with care, name what is there honestly, and offer you something practical to do — small, dignified, within your means. If, after our conversation, you leave with even a little more clarity than you came with, I have done my job.”
